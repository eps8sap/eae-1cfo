Cash Flow Management

Cash flow is the single most important financial metric for a growing business. Profitable companies fail when they run out of cash. Effective cash flow management ensures you have the liquidity to meet obligations, invest in growth, and weather unexpected disruptions.

Cash Flow Forecasting

13-Week Cash Flow Forecast

The 13-week cash flow forecast is the foundational tool for short-term liquidity management. Updated weekly, it provides a rolling view of expected cash inflows and outflows, helping you anticipate shortfalls and make proactive decisions.

Monthly Cash Flow Projections

For medium-term planning, monthly cash flow projections extend the view to 6–12 months. These integrate with your budget and forecast to show the cash impact of your financial plan.

Scenario-Based Cash Planning

What happens if your largest customer pays 30 days late? What if revenue drops 20%? Scenario analysis stress-tests your cash position against realistic risks, ensuring you have contingency plans.

Cash Management Best Practices

  • Maintain a cash reserve of 3–6 months of operating expenses
  • Establish a line of credit before you need it
  • Review cash position weekly, not just monthly
  • Separate operating cash from reserves and restricted funds
  • Automate recurring payments to avoid late fees and maintain vendor trust
