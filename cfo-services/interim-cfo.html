Interim CFO Services

An interim CFO provides immediate, experienced financial leadership during periods of transition. Whether your CFO resigned, you are navigating an acquisition, or you need senior financial talent during a critical growth phase, an interim CFO brings stability and expertise when you need it most.

When You Need an Interim CFO

  • CFO Departure: Your CFO left and you need someone in the seat while you search for a permanent replacement
  • Fundraising or M&A: You need experienced financial leadership to manage a capital raise, acquisition, or exit
  • Financial Turnaround: Cash flow problems, covenant violations, or operational restructuring requiring immediate attention
  • Rapid Growth: Your business is scaling faster than your financial infrastructure can support
  • IPO Preparation: You need someone who has been through the public offering process

How Interim CFO Engagements Work

Phase 1: Rapid Assessment (Week 1–2)

The interim CFO conducts a comprehensive assessment of your financial position, team capabilities, systems, and immediate risks. They deliver a situation report with prioritized action items.

Phase 2: Stabilization (Weeks 3–8)

Address urgent issues: cash flow gaps, reporting backlogs, compliance deadlines, or stakeholder concerns. Establish reliable financial processes and reporting cadences.

Phase 3: Optimization (Months 3–6)

With the foundation stable, the interim CFO focuses on strategic improvements: better forecasting, improved metrics, system upgrades, or team development.

Phase 4: Transition (Final Month)

Comprehensive handoff to your permanent CFO or ongoing fractional engagement. Documentation of processes, relationships, and in-progress initiatives.

Interim CFO vs. Other Models

The key difference is intensity and duration. An interim CFO typically works full-time (or close to it) for a defined period of 3–12 months. Compare this to a fractional CFO who works part-time on an ongoing basis, or a consultant who addresses specific projects.

What to Expect

  • Speed: An experienced interim CFO can be operational within days, not weeks
  • Objectivity: Fresh perspective unencumbered by internal politics
  • Experience: Most interim CFOs have led finance functions at multiple companies and have navigated the exact situation you are facing
  • Flexibility: Engagement scales to match your needs and concludes when the job is done
